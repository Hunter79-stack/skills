## Cultural Etiquette

### Do's âœ…

- **Accept things with both hands** â€” Business cards, gifts, money
- **Toast lower** â€” When clinking glasses, keep yours lower than elders/seniors
- **Try everything offered** â€” Refusing food is impolite
- **Carry tissues** â€” Public toilets often don't have paper
- **Learn basic Chinese** â€” Even "hello" and "thank you" appreciated
- **Be patient with crowds** â€” Personal space concept is different
- **Respect photography rules** â€” Many temples ban photos inside

### Don'ts âŒ

- **Don't tip** â€” Not expected, can be awkward
- **Don't discuss politics** â€” Taiwan, Tibet, Tiananmen â€” avoid completely
- **Don't stick chopsticks upright** â€” Resembles funeral incense
- **Don't give clocks as gifts** â€” "Giving clock" sounds like "attending funeral"
- **Don't write names in red** â€” Associated with death
- **Don't expect queuing** â€” Pushing is normal (improving but still...)
- **Don't be surprised by staring** â€” Foreigners attract attention outside tourist areas

### Restaurant Culture

- **Sharing:** Dishes are communal, placed in center
- **Lazy Susan:** Spin to share dishes
- **Rice:** Often comes at the END (it's a filler)
- **Bones/shells:** Spit onto plate or table â€” normal
- **Fighting for the bill:** Host pays, guests fight to pay = respect
- **Tipping:** Not expected, not necessary
