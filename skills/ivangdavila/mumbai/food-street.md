# Mumbai Street Food & Local Classics

## The Street Food Culture

Street food isn't just cheap eats in Mumbai — it's a way of life. From breakfast to 3am post-party snacks, streets are the city's true restaurants.

---

## Essential Street Foods

### Vada Pav (₹15-40)
Mumbai's burger. Deep-fried spiced potato ball (vada) in bread (pav).

**Components:**
- Vada: Spiced mashed potato, gram flour batter, deep-fried
- Pav: Soft bread roll
- Chutneys: Green (cilantro), red (garlic), dry garlic
- Fried chili: Essential accompaniment

**Best spots:**
| Name | Location | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Ashok Vada Pav | Dadar Station | Legendary, massive queue |
| Anand Stall | Vile Parle | Consistent favorite |
| Gajanan | Thane | Worth the trip |
| Jumbo King | Chain | Reliable, quick |

**Tip:** Ask for extra "sukhi chutney" (dry garlic chutney)

---

### Pav Bhaji (₹60-150)
Invented in Mumbai for textile mill workers. Mashed vegetables in spiced tomato gravy, served with buttered bread.

**What makes it great:**
- Obscene amounts of butter
- Slightly crispy pav
- Raw onion and lemon on side
- Completely mashed (no chunks)

**Best spots:**
| Name | Location | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Sardar Refreshments | Tardeo | The original, best |
| Cannon Pav Bhaji | Chowpatty | Beachside classic |
| Amar Juice Centre | Vile Parle | Late night legend |
| Guru Kripa | Sion | Office worker favorite |

**When to eat:** Best as dinner or late-night snack

---

### Pani Puri / Golgappa (₹30-60)
Hollow crisp puris filled with spiced water and filling. The signature street snack.

**The experience:**
- Stand at stall
- Vendor hands you filled puris one by one
- Eat whole in one bite (crucial)
- Repeat 6-8 times
- Say "bas" (enough) when done

**Best spots:**
| Name | Location | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Elco Market | Bandra | Clean, consistent |
| Guru Kripa | Multiple | Reliable chain |
| Chowpatty stalls | Marine Drive | Classic experience |
| Kailash Parbat | Multiple | Restaurant-style |

**Tip:** Ask for "kam teekha" (less spicy) if worried about spice

---

### Bhel Puri (₹40-80)
Puffed rice, sev (crispy noodles), chutneys, vegetables — mixed tableside.

**Variations:**
- **Bhel Puri**: Puffed rice based
- **Sev Puri**: On flat crisp rounds
- **Dahi Puri**: With yogurt
- **Ragda Pattice**: With potato patties and curry

**Best:** Elco, Chowpatty, any beach/station vendor

---

### Bombay Sandwich (₹40-100)
Grilled sandwich with vegetables, cheese, and mint chutney. Mumbai's answer to fast food.

**Standard fillings:**
- Potato slices, cucumber, tomato, beetroot
- Cheese (Amul)
- Mint-cilantro chutney
- Sometimes corn, paneer

**Best spots:**
- Station sandwichwallas (any major station)
- Popular near colleges
- 24-hour options at many spots

**Order:** "Cheese sandwich, grilled, butter mein" (cheese, grilled, with butter)

---

### Keema Pav (₹80-150)
Spiced minced meat served with bread. Irani cafe specialty.

**Best spots:**
| Name | Location | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Olympia | Colaba | Classic, no-frills |
| Kayani | Dhobi Talao | Perfect with chai |
| Sassanian | Dhobi Talao | Old school |

---

### Misal Pav (₹60-120)
Maharashtrian specialty. Spicy sprouted moth beans curry with farsan (crunchy toppings).

**Spice levels:**
- Mumbai misal: Medium
- Kolhapuri misal: Very spicy
- Puneri misal: Milder

**Best spots:**
| Name | Location | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Aaswad | Dadar | Most famous |
| Mamledar Misal | Thane | Legendary |
| Bedekar | Girgaum | Old school |

**Warning:** Don't underestimate the spice. Start mild.

---

### Dabeli (₹30-50)
Gujarati-origin snack. Spiced potato filling with peanuts, pomegranate, in bread.

**Best spots:**
- Swati Snacks (Tardeo)
- Juice/snack stalls near stations

---

### Frankie / Kathi Roll (₹60-120)
Mumbai's wrap. Flatbread rolled with filling (veg or non-veg).

**Options:**
- Paneer tikka frankie
- Chicken tikka frankie
- Egg frankie
- Keema frankie

**Best:** 
- Tibbs Frankie (chain, original brand)
- Any "Frankie" stall

## Non-Veg Street Food

### Mohammed Ali Road Area
Mumbai's non-veg street food heaven. Best during Ramadan.

**Must-try:**
| Item | What It Is | Price |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Seekh Kebab | Minced meat on skewers | ₹100-200 |
| Boti Kebab | Marinated meat pieces | ₹150-250 |
| Nalli Nihari | Slow-cooked bone marrow stew | ₹150-300 |
| Khiri (Udder) | Specialty, not for all | ₹100-150 |
| Malpua | Sweet pancake dessert | ₹50-100 |

**Best spots:**
- Sarvi
- Noor Mohammadi
- Suleman Usman Mithaiwala (desserts)

**When:** Evening onwards (shops open after 6pm mostly)

---

### Bademiya

**Must-try:**

## Street Food Safety Guide

### DO:
- Eat where there's a crowd (high turnover = fresh)
- Watch food being made fresh
- Carry hand sanitizer
- Start with less spicy options
- Eat at established spots first

### DON'T:
- Drink tap water
- Eat raw salads from street stalls
- Eat from empty stalls (no turnover)
- Assume your stomach will adjust immediately

### Building Tolerance:
1. **Days 1-3**: Restaurant food, cooked items only
2. **Days 4-7**: Add established street food (Elco, branded)
3. **Week 2+**: Adventure freely

## Finding Street Food

- **Train stations**: Always have stalls
- **Beach areas**: Chowpatty, Juhu
- **College areas**: Cheap, good
- **Markets**: Crawford, Linking Road
- **Late night**: Outside bars/pubs

Best discovery method: Ask locals "Where do YOU eat?"
