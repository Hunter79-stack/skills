# Mumbai Itineraries

## 1 Day: Mumbai Essentials

Best for: Quick stopover or first-time visitor wanting highlights.

### Morning (7am - 12pm)
1. **Gateway of India** (7:00-7:45am)
   - Arrive early to beat crowds
   - Walk around, take photos
   - See Taj Mahal Palace Hotel from outside

2. **Breakfast at Leopold Cafe** (8:00-8:45am)
   - Historic cafe since 1871
   - Full breakfast, good coffee
   - ₹500-800 for breakfast

3. **Colaba Causeway Walk** (9:00-10:30am)
   - Browse shops (most open by 10am)
   - Street photography
   - Buy souvenirs if needed

4. **Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST)** (11:00-11:45am)
   - Take Uber/taxi (30 min from Colaba)
   - UNESCO World Heritage site
   - Beautiful Victorian Gothic architecture

### Afternoon (12pm - 6pm)
5. **Crawford Market** (12:00-1:00pm)
   - 5-minute walk from CST
   - Experience local market life
   - Grab fresh fruit

6. **Lunch at Britannia & Co** (1:15-2:15pm)
   - Iconic Parsi restaurant since 1923
   - Try Berry Pulao (signature dish)
   - ₹800-1,200 for lunch

7. **CSMVS Museum** (2:30-4:30pm)
   - Art and history
   - Air-conditioned break from heat
   - ₹85 entry (₹500 for foreigners)

8. **Dhobi Ghat Quick Visit** (5:00-5:30pm)
   - View from Mahalaxmi station bridge
   - Quick photo stop
   - Free

### Evening (6pm - 10pm)
9. **Marine Drive Sunset** (6:00-7:30pm)
   - Walk the promenade
   - Watch sunset over Arabian Sea
   - See Art Deco buildings

10. **Chowpatty Beach** (7:30-8:30pm)
    - End of Marine Drive
    - Street food: Pav Bhaji, Bhel Puri
    - ₹100-200 for snacks

11. **Dinner at Trishna** (9:00-10:00pm)
    - Famous seafood restaurant
    - Butter Garlic Crab is legendary
    - ₹2,000-3,000 for dinner

## 3 Days: Comprehensive Mumbai

### Day 1: South Mumbai Heritage

**Morning**
- 7:00am: Gateway of India (early, less crowded)
- 8:00am: Breakfast at Taj Mahal Palace (Sea Lounge)
- 9:30am: Walk to Colaba Causeway
- 11:00am: CST Railway Station
- 12:00pm: Crawford Market

**Afternoon**
- 1:00pm: Lunch at Britannia (Berry Pulao)
- 2:30pm: Fort area walk (Horniman Circle, Flora Fountain)
- 4:00pm: Kala Ghoda art galleries
- 5:30pm: CSMVS Museum

**Evening**
- 7:00pm: Marine Drive sunset walk
- 8:30pm: Dinner at Khyber (Mughlai) or Trishna (Seafood)

### Day 2: Culture & Local Life

**Morning**
- 7:00am: Dhobi Ghat (morning wash activity)
- 8:30am: Siddhivinayak Temple
- 10:30am: Mahalaxmi Temple + Haji Ali Dargah (check tide)

**Afternoon**
- 1:00pm: Lunch at Cafe Noorani (iconic Mughlai)
- 2:30pm: Dharavi tour (book Reality Tours in advance)
- 5:30pm: Bandra exploring (Bandstand, Linking Road)

**Evening**
- 7:00pm: Carter Road sunset, Bandra
- 8:30pm: Dinner in Bandra (Bastian, The Table, or Pali Village Cafe)
- 10:00pm: Bar hopping in Bandra if interested

### Day 3: Islands & Hidden Gems

**Full Morning**
- 7:00am: Ferry to Elephanta Caves (from Gateway)
- Explore caves (2 hours on island)
- Return by 1:00pm

**Afternoon**
- 1:30pm: Lunch at Mahesh Lunch Home (seafood)
- 3:00pm: Chor Bazaar (antique hunting)
- 5:00pm: Banganga Tank (ancient, peaceful)
- 6:30pm: Malabar Hill & Hanging Gardens

**Evening**
- 7:30pm: Sunset from Kamala Nehru Park
- 8:30pm: Final dinner — splurge at Masala Library or Indian Accent

## Practical Notes

### Daily Budget
- **Budget**: ₹3,000-5,000/day ($35-60)
- **Mid-range**: ₹8,000-15,000/day ($95-180)
- **Luxury**: ₹25,000+/day ($300+)

### Getting Around
- **Within South Mumbai**: Walk or taxi
- **South to Bandra**: Uber/Ola (₹300-500) or train (₹15)
- **Local trains**: Avoid 8-10am and 5-8pm

### Booking Ahead
- Dharavi tours: 2-3 days
- Film City: 1 week
- Elephanta: Show up early (first ferry 9am)
- Popular restaurants: Reserve dinner
