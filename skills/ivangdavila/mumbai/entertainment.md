# Bollywood & Entertainment Industry

## Overview

Mumbai is **the entertainment capital of India**:
- Bollywood (Hindi film industry) — world's largest by output
- Television production
- Music industry
- Advertising and media
- OTT content creation

---

## Bollywood Basics

### Scale
- 1,500-2,000 films/year (all Indian languages)
- Hindi films: 300-400/year
- Global audience: 3+ billion
- Revenue: $2.5+ billion annually

## Working in Entertainment

### Job Types

| Role | Entry Path | Pay Range (Monthly) |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| Actor (extra) | Casting agencies | ₹500-2,000/day |
| Actor (supporting) | Auditions, networking | ₹10,000-1,00,000/project |
| Actor (lead, TV) | Auditions | ₹50,000-5,00,000 |
| Actor (lead, film) | Years of work | ₹10L-10Cr+ per film |
| Director (assistant) | Film school, assisting | ₹20,000-50,000 |
| Writer | Scripts, networking | ₹30,000-2,00,000 |
| Cinematographer (assistant) | Film school | ₹25,000-50,000 |
| Editor | Software skills | ₹30,000-1,00,000 |
| Production assistant | Entry point | ₹15,000-30,000 |

### Getting Started

**For Actors:**
1. Join acting classes (Anupam Kher's, Jeff Goldberg, etc.)
2. Register with casting agencies
3. Build portfolio (photos, showreel)
4. Start with ads, TV serials
5. Network relentlessly

**For Crew:**
1. Film school helps (FTII, Whistling Woods)
2. Or assist established professionals
3. Build portfolio
4. Graduate to bigger projects

## Breaking In: Reality Check

### The Hard Truth
- Extremely competitive
- Connections matter (a lot)
- Financial runway needed
- Rejection is constant
- Years before stability

### What Helps
- Talent + persistence
- Financial cushion (2-3 years)
- Good portfolio
- Training/skill development
- Networking (genuine)
- Luck

### Red Flags
- Anyone asking for money upfront
- "Guaranteed" roles
- Casting couch (report to authorities)
- Unregistered productions

## Living as Entertainment Worker

### Best Areas
- Versova (aspiring artists)
- Andheri West (working professionals)
- Bandra (established)
- Oshiwara (TV industry)

### Reality
- Irregular income (freelance)
- Long hours on sets
- Late nights common
- Social life through industry

### Budget (Starting Out)
| Expense | Monthly |
|---------|---------|
| Rent (shared, Andheri) | ₹15,000-20,000 |
| Living expenses | ₹20,000-30,000 |
| Classes/portfolio | ₹10,000 |
| Total | ₹45,000-60,000 |

**Need**: 1-2 years of savings before trying
