# Markets and Food Halls — Sevilla

## Traditional Markets

### Mercado de Triana
**Best market in Sevilla**

| Info | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Location** | Plaza del Altozano, Triana |
| **Hours** | 09:00-15:00, some stalls evening |
| **Vibe** | Mix of traditional and modern |
| **Best for** | Fresh food, tapas, breakfast |

**What's inside:**
- Fresh produce
- Fish stalls
- Meat/charcuteria
- Cheese
- Prepared food stalls
- Tapas bars (several inside)

**Recommended:**
- **Breakfast**: Tostada con tomate at any bar
- **Tapas**: Multiple bars inside, quality varies
- **Shopping**: Fresh fish, vegetables, olives

---

### Mercado del Arenal
**Tourist-local mix, near bullring**

| Info | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Location** | Calle Pastor y Landero, Arenal |
| **Hours** | 09:00-15:00 |
| **Vibe** | Traditional with some tourist stalls |

## When to Visit Markets

### Best Times
- **Traditional markets**: 09:00-12:00 (freshest, busiest)
- **Gourmet halls**: 13:00-15:00 or 20:00-22:00
- **Sunday flea market**: 09:00-12:00

### Avoid
- **Mondays**: Some stalls closed
- **After 14:00**: Traditional markets closing
- **August**: Reduced hours/closures

---

## Market Eating Tips

### How to Eat at Markets

**Traditional markets:**
1. Find the bar area inside
2. Order at bar (standing)
3. Point at what looks good
4. Pay when leaving

**Gourmet halls:**
1. Walk around, choose stall
2. Order at counter
3. Find seat
4. Return for more if hungry

### What to Order

| Market Type | Good Choices |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Traditional** | Tortilla, fried fish, jamon |
| **Gourmet** | Depends on stall — quality varies |

## Practical Information

### Payment
- **Traditional markets**: Cash often preferred, cards sometimes
- **Gourmet halls**: Cards accepted everywhere

### Language
- **Traditional markets**: Spanish essential
- **Gourmet halls**: Some English

### Bags
- **Bring your own**: Plastic bags less common now
- **Baskets**: Some markets provide

## Recommended Experiences

### For Foodies
1. **Mercado de Triana** breakfast
2. **Lonja del Barranco** evening tapas
3. **Sunday flea market** (Feria) atmosphere

### For Tourists
1. **Mercado de Triana** (accessible, quality)
2. **Lonja del Barranco** (comfortable)
3. **Mercado Encarnacion** + Setas combo

### For Locals/Long-term
1. **Mercado de la Feria** (cheapest, realest)
2. **Any neighborhood market** for daily shopping
3. **Sunday flea market** for treasures
