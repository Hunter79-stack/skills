# Traditional Sevillian Food

## Cold Soups (Summer Essentials)

### Gazpacho
Cold tomato soup. The original Spanish summer staple.

**Ingredients:** Tomatoes, cucumber, pepper, garlic, bread, olive oil, vinegar
**Served:** Cold, often with diced vegetable garnish
**When:** Summer (Apr-Oct), but available year-round

| Where | Location | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Most restaurants | Everywhere | Standard menu item |
| Mercado de Triana | Triana | Fresh, cheap |
| Taberna del Alabardero | Centro | Upscale version |

**Sevillian style:** Thinner than Cordoba, more vinegar tang.

---

### Salmorejo
Thicker, creamier than gazpacho. Cordoban origin but beloved in Sevilla.

**Ingredients:** Tomatoes, bread, garlic, olive oil (no cucumber, pepper)
**Served:** Cold, topped with chopped jamon and boiled egg
**When:** Summer primarily

| Where | Location | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Bar Las Teresas | Santa Cruz | Classic version |
| El Rinconcillo | Centro | Historic bar |
| Most tapas bars | Everywhere | Standard summer tapa |

---

## Fried Fish (Pescaito Frito)

Sevilla's most popular tapa category.

### Essential Fried Fish

| Fish | What | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Boquerones fritos** | Fried anchovies | Most common, eaten whole |
| **Chocos fritos** | Fried cuttlefish | Tender, mild, popular |
| **Cazón en adobo** | Marinated shark | Cubed, fried, iconic |
| **Puntillitas** | Baby squid | Crispy, pile of tentacles |
| **Gambas al ajillo** | Garlic prawns | Not fried but essential |
| **Fritura mixta** | Mixed fry | Best sampler |

### Best Fried Fish Places

| Bar | Location | Known For |
|-----|----------|-----------|
| **Freiduria La Isla** | Triana | Takeaway fish legends |
| **Casa Manolo** | Triana | Classic fritura |
| **La Primera del Puente** | Centro | River views + fish |
| **El Kiosco de las Flores** | Triana | Riverside fritura |

**Eating style:** Squeeze lemon, eat with fingers, small fork optional.

## Pork and Jamon

Jamon (cured ham) is religion here.

### Jamon Types

| Type | Quality | Price |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Jamon serrano** | Standard cured ham | EUR 3-5/tapa |
| **Jamon iberico** | Iberian pig, acorn-fed | EUR 8-15/tapa |
| **Jamon de bellota** | Top grade iberico | EUR 15-25/tapa |

### Pork Dishes

| Dish | What | Where |
|------|------|-------|
| **Carrillada** | Pork cheeks, slow braised | Most tapas bars |
| **Solomillo al whisky** | Pork loin in whisky sauce | Classic Sevillian |
| **Presa iberica** | Iberian pork shoulder | Upscale bars |
| **Secreto iberico** | Fatty pork cut, grilled | Meat-focused bars |
| **Pringa** | Slow-cooked meat mix | Traditional sandwich |

### Jamon Experience

For quality jamon sliced fresh:
| Place | Location | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **El Rinconcillo** | Centro | Historic, great jamon |
| **Casa Morales** | Centro | Classic bar |

## Eggs and Tortilla

### Tortilla Espanola
Potato omelette. The most ordered tapa in Spain.

**Versions:**
- **Jugosa** (runny center) — Traditional Sevilla style
- **Cuajada** (fully cooked) — Safer, drier
- **Con cebolla** (with onion) — Most common

| Where | Location | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Bar Alfalfa** | Centro | Famous tortilla |
| **Bodeguita Romero** | Centro | Classic |
| **Most neighborhood bars** | Everywhere | Daily staple |

### Huevos Rotos
Fried eggs over fried potatoes, runny yolk breaks over everything. Often with jamon or chorizo.

## Stews and Heavy Dishes

### Espinacas con Garbanzos
Spinach with chickpeas. Arabic-influenced Sevillian classic.

**Note:** Vegetarian but check — some versions have ham.

| Where | Location |
|-------|----------|
| **El Rinconcillo** | Centro |
| **Bodega Santa Cruz** | Santa Cruz |
| **Casa Roman** | Centro |

### Rabo de Toro
Oxtail stew. Rich, gelatinous, deeply flavored.

**Best in autumn/winter.**

### Cocido
Chickpea stew with meats and vegetables. Winter comfort food.

### Callos
Tripe stew. Not for everyone but traditional.

## Street Food and Quick Bites

### Montaditos
Small toasts with various toppings. Chain 100 Montaditos made them famous but traditional ones are better.

### Serranito
THE Sevillian sandwich:
- Grilled pork loin
- Serrano ham
- Green pepper (fried)
- Tomato
- Bread roll

**Essential after a night out or during Feria.**

| Where | Location |
|-------|----------|
| **Bar Europa** | Triana |
| **Pali Pali** | Centro |
| **Most neighborhood bars** | Everywhere |

### Flamenquin
Rolled pork loin wrapped in jamon, breaded and fried. Cordoban but everywhere.

## Desserts

### Tocino de Cielo
Sevillian egg yolk caramel custard. Intensely sweet, rich.

### Torrijas
Spanish french toast. Lent/Easter specialty.

### Pestiños
Fried dough with honey. Arabic influence.

### Polvorones and Mantecados
Crumbly shortbread cookies. Christmas specialty, available year-round.

| Where | Location |
|-------|----------|
| **La Campana** | Centro | Famous pastry shop |
| **Horno San Buenaventura** | Centro | Traditional bakery |

## Drinks

### Beer
- **Cruzcampo** — Local Sevillian beer, most common
- **Alhambra** — Granadan alternative, popular
- **Mahou** — Madrid beer, also everywhere
- **Order:** "Una cana" for small beer

### Wine
- **Fino** — Dry sherry from Jerez (30 min away)
- **Manzanilla** — Lighter sherry from Sanlucar
- **Tinto** — Red wine, often from Rioja
- **Tinto de verano** — Red wine + lemon soda (summer standard)

### Other
- **Rebujito** — Fino + 7UP (Feria drink)
- **Cerveza con limon** — Beer + lemon soda (Clara)
- **Cafe con hielo** — Iced coffee (espresso + ice glass)
- **Vermut** — Growing trend, pre-meal

---

## Seasonal Specials

| Season | Dishes |
|--------|--------|
| **Spring (Feria)** | Rebujito, pescaito, jamon, everything festive |
| **Summer** | Gazpacho, salmorejo, cold soups, lots of fish |
| **Autumn** | Stews return, mushrooms (setas), game |
| **Winter** | Cocido, rabo de toro, heavy meats |
| **Semana Santa** | Torrijas, bacalao, no meat on certain days |
