## Free Tapas Bars

Yes, they still exist. Order a drink, get a free tapa.

| Bar | Area | Quality |
|-----|------|---------|
| **Various Macarena bars** | Macarena | Basic but free |
| **Calle Feria bars** | Feria | Good |
| **Some Triana backstreets** | Triana | Varies |

**How it works:**
1. Order cana or wine
2. Bartender brings small tapa
3. No choosing — you get what they have
4. Each drink = new tapa
5. Move to next bar

## Tapas Etiquette

### Ordering
- "Una caña" (small beer) standard
- "Quiero..." (I want...) + point if needed
- No need to order everything at once
- Pay at end

### Standing vs Sitting
- **Barra (bar)**: Faster, often cheaper, authentic
- **Mesa (table)**: More space, sometimes more expensive
- **Terraza**: Premium pricing (10-20% more)

### Sharing
- **Raciones** meant for sharing
- Point at what others have if you want it
- No one will judge

### When to Go
- **13:30-15:00**: Pre-lunch tapas
- **20:30-22:30**: Prime time
- **Empty at 21:00**: Probably tourist trap

---

## Avoiding Tourist Traps

### Red Flags
- Photos of food outside
- "Tourist menu" signs
- Empty at 22:00
- Located directly at major monument
- Hawkers pulling you in
- Prices not posted

### Green Flags
- Crowded at 21:30+
- Standing locals at bar
- Handwritten specials
- TV showing football
- Old decor
- No English menu (or bad translation)

## Practical Tips

### How Much to Eat
- 3-4 tapas/person for light meal
- 5-6 tapas/person if hungry
- 2-3 bars per night typical

### Timing
- Kitchen closes: varies (23:00-00:00 typical)
- Peak crowding: 21:00-22:30
- Beat crowds: go at 20:30

### Reservations
- Most tapas bars: not needed
- Eslava: recommended
- Weekend dinner: helpful at popular spots
